Taste Matching Model - Methods and Choices (Simple Explanation)

1) Why TruncatedSVD?
- We do not have explicit user ratings/labels for "good match" vs "bad match".
- TruncatedSVD learns hidden taste dimensions from audio-feature patterns automatically.
- It compresses noisy 8-feature vectors into a smaller latent space that keeps the most useful structure.

2) Why unsupervised training?
- Supervised learning needs ground-truth labels (for example, many known compatible and incompatible user pairs).
- This project does not have large, reliable labeled pair data.
- Unsupervised training lets us learn from the full catalog immediately.

3) Why cosine similarity in latent space?
- Cosine focuses on taste direction/pattern, not absolute magnitude.
- It is robust for comparing preference profiles built from multiple tracks.

4) How we evaluated "accuracy" without labels
- Baseline agreement: compare model similarity to raw-feature similarity on synthetic user playlists.
- Neighborhood preservation: compare Top-K nearest neighbors in raw vs latent spaces.
- Lower error + higher overlap means the latent model keeps meaningful taste structure.

5) Results summary (this run)
- Pairwise Pearson correlation: 0.9466
- Pairwise Spearman correlation: 0.9472
- Similarity MAE (0-100 scale): 5.5132
- Neighbor recall@10: 0.4590
- Explained variance captured by latent model: 0.8334

Interpretation:
- Higher correlations and recall indicate the model preserves core taste relationships.
- Lower MAE indicates closer agreement with the baseline similarity geometry.
